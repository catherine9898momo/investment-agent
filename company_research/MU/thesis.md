# Micron Technology (MU) Research Thesis

## Boundary

This is a company research note, not a buy, sell, hold, short, add, trim, or allocation recommendation.

## Investment Thesis

I study MU not because it dropped about 6% today, but because AI and HBM may be changing memory from a traditional cyclical commodity into a strategic bottleneck asset. The hard question is whether the market is still discounting MU as a cycle stock, or has already priced in a durable AI memory supercycle.

## Current Core Judgment

MU is a good company in a very strong cycle, and it is being re-rated around AI/HBM. The thesis is supported by record revenue, record margin, record free cash flow, HBM4 progress, strategic customer agreements, and tight supply. The risk is equally clear: the market may be treating peak or near-peak profitability as structurally permanent.

## Company Quality

| Question | Current View |
|---|---|
| What does it earn money from? | DRAM, NAND, HBM, SSDs, and related memory/storage products. FY2026 Q2 DRAM revenue was about $18.8B, 79% of revenue; NAND revenue was about $5.0B, 21% of revenue. |
| Profit source | FY2026 Q2 profit was driven mainly by higher pricing, tight supply, product mix, and execution. |
| Moat | Technology nodes, yield, manufacturing scale, customer qualification, HBM roadmap, advanced packaging, and strategic customer agreements. |
| Pricing power | Very strong right now, but it is still partly cycle-driven pricing power. DRAM ASP rose in the mid-60% range sequentially in FY2026 Q2; NAND ASP rose in the high-70% range. |
| Capital intensity | Very high. FY2026 capex is expected to exceed $25B, with FY2027 equipment spend expected to rise. |
| Management | Current capital allocation looks disciplined: debt reduction, net cash, dividend increase, and growth capex. The future test is whether 2027-2028 supply growth remains disciplined. |

## Industry Cycle

| Question | Current View |
|---|---|
| Demand source | AI data centers, HBM, server DRAM, data center SSDs, traditional server refresh, AI PCs, smartphones, auto, and embedded. |
| Supply release timing | New supply is slow. Idaho initial wafer output is expected around mid-2027; Singapore NAND output is expected in the second half of 2028. |
| HBM position | HBM4 volume shipments have begun; HBM4E is targeted for 2027. HBM consumes DRAM wafer capacity and can improve value per bit and customer stickiness. |
| Inventory | FY2026 Q2 ending inventory was $8.3B, with 123 days of inventory. DRAM inventory days were below 120 days and described as tight. |
| Expansion risk | Micron, Samsung, and SK hynix are all economically incentivized to add capacity. The key risk is whether 2027-2028 capacity growth overwhelms AI demand. |

## Valuation Frame

| Scenario | Normalized EPS Thought | Multiple Thought | Interpretation |
|---|---:|---:|---|
| Bear/downcycle | 15-25 | 8-12x | Traditional memory cycle valuation; current price would be fragile. |
| Strong normalized cycle | 35-50 | 12-18x | Current valuation requires a long high-profit cycle. |
| AI structural re-rate | 60-80+ | 15-22x | Current price can be rational if high HBM/DRAM profitability persists. |
| Bubble risk | Peak EPS treated as permanent | 20x+ | Main risk is valuation compression even if near-term earnings remain strong. |

The key valuation question is not whether FY2026 forward PE looks low. It is whether FY2027-FY2029 normalized EPS and free cash flow stay structurally elevated after capex and new supply.

## Falsification Conditions

- HBM, DRAM, or NAND pricing starts loosening, especially if contract and spot prices weaken together.
- Customer strategic agreements fail to support stable volume, pricing, or capacity planning.
- Samsung, SK hynix, and Micron capacity additions create 2027-2028 oversupply.
- Hyperscaler AI capex or GPU/HBM order expectations are revised down.
- Micron gross margin falls quickly from the 75%-80% area toward historical DRAM cycle levels.
- Free cash flow is absorbed by capex, leaving strong EPS but weak distributable cash generation.
- Chinese memory producers pressure mid/low-end DRAM or NAND pricing enough to affect the broader cycle.
- Market narrative shifts from "AI memory bottleneck" back to "memory cycle peak."
